Depression

Global study shows that one in four people in the general population experience some level of depression. Rate of depression is found to be 3X times the pre-pandemic level. People under extreme pressure can have more exposure to Coronavirus like university students, frontline health workers. The rate of mental illness is even greater. 50% of COVID patients were found to be experiencing depression and anexiety.

Bereavement

About 5 million deaths occured during COVID-19 resulting loss of many loved ones in many families worldwide. People lost their special ones like parents, siblings, family and friends. These demises were difficult to cope up. People became alone and had to live in brief of their loved ones. This resulted in a feeling of Bereavement among a large population globally.

Delirium

Delirium, or a confused mental state, occurs suddenly. A person has a change in mental status and acts disoriented and distracted. Delirium is more common in older adults, especially those with dementia, and people who need hospitalization. Delirium was a complex mental state caused among people due to extreme mental pressure that resulted in distraction in work.

Isolation

Isolation, after the social distancing norm, became a popular way of living. People who were even the extroverts, started living alone, this encapsulated a feeling of loneliness and fear among them. Isolation was an abundantly found mental problem that everyone dealt with. People had no one to talk during the tough time. So it was really difficult for all to deal with loneliness.

Alcohol & Drugs

People who were unable to cope up with changing mental situations started taking Alcohol and drugs abundantly. This increased health issues among them. People, in order to run away from mental stress, took alcohol, and invited many other complications like strokes.
